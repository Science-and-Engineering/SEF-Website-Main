Aberdeen

Public Transportation

  • Grasshopper Pass
  • More on Aberdeen Transportation
  • Walking! Walking is a great way to travel through Aberdeen because of the small size of the city.

Outdoor Attractions

  • Duthie Park: A large park with with gardens and playgrounds for kids.
  • Hazlehead Park: A public park with sporting grounds, playgrounds, walking trails, and a cultural sculpture gallery.
  • Aberdeen Beach: This is a beautiful beach that is a hub for surfers and is famous for its golden colored sand.
  • Aberdeen Harbor: This is a vibrant colored harbor that also has a economic importance due to it serving as a ferry route between Orkney and Shetland.
  • Den of Maindencraig: This is a nature reserve that has wonderful scenery and a lot of fauna to awe at.
  • Donmouth Local Nature Reserve: This is a calm and quiet beach that allows you to see nature undisturbed.
  • Pitmedden Garden: This garden is meticulously maintained, allowing it to have a brilliant design and dazzling colors.
  • North East Falconry Centre: This center allows you to get up close with one of nature's birds of prey, the falcon.

Indoor and Historical Attractions

  • Aberdeen Maritime Museum: An nteractive museum that illustrates Aberdeen's heritage and North sea oil industry.
  • The Tolbooth Musuem: One of the best preserves jails in all of Scotland, The Tolbooth Museum provides information through the use of displays on Aberdonian crime and punishment.
  • Balmoral Castle: summer castle for Queen Elizabeth, and has beautiful gardens and historical monuments. Tour guides and tour phones are available upon purchase. Great for history lovers.
  • Aberdeen Art Gallery: This art gallery has a comprehensive set of paintings made by people such as Raeburn and Monet.
  • Craigievar Castle: This famously pink castle has a vast collection of paintings, armors, and weapons that are available for public viewing.
  • Torry Battery: Torry Battery provides wonderful scenery of the Aberdeen harbor while also being built in 1860 to serve the purpose of housing a defence and hospital base in various wars.
  • Crathie Church: This small church is the regular place of worship by the Royal Family when visiting Balmoral Castle and showcases stained glass window, bells, and a communion table.
  • Gordon Highlanders Museum: army history museum that contains maps, uniforms, and the weapons of the 200 year old Scottish Army.

Glasgow

Public Transportation

  • Buses, National road services, Ferries, and the Glasgow Subway make up Glasgow public transportation.

Outdoor Attractions

  • Pollok Country Park: This park is known for its scenic walking trails and its wonderfully maintained green lawns.
  • Kelvingrove Park: This spacious and attractive park host numerous festivals and events all throughout the year.
  • Glasgow Botanic Gardens: This unique and modern Botanic Garden showcases beautiful flora and fauna.
  • Glasgow West End: This is a cosmopolitan hub in Glasgow that showcases amazing wildlife and some of Glasgow’s finest scenery.
  • River Clyde: This is a beautiful river that is the eight longest river of the UK.
  • Grascadden Nature Reserve: This woodland emanates tranquility and beauty with its majestic oaks and hawthorne hedges.
  • Lenzie Moss Nature Reserve: This is a large area of outdoor space which allows people to saunter and enjoy nature.
  • George Square: This is Glasgow's top civic square that is filled with detailed statues of famous Scots.

Indoor and Historical Attractions

  • Glasgow Police Museum: This is a highly rated museum that showcases the history and the many facets of the Glasgow Police Force.
  • Hunterian Museum: The Hunterian is Scotland’s oldest museum that is home to millions of historically and scientifically significant items.
  • Jurassic Jungle: This is a unique adventure that is aimed towards educating the youth more about life 65 million years ago.
  • Scotland Street School Museum: This is a architecturally beautiful museum located at the heart of Glasgow that serves as an information center of Scottish education.
  • Burrell Collection: Situated on the southside of Glasgow, Burrell Collection houses famous artwork from the likes of Rodin, Degas, and more.
  • Provand's Lordship: This medieval house museum showcases royal portraits, 17th-century furniture, and amazing brickwork.
  • The Necropolis: This historically significant Victorian era cemetery is worth exploring, as it has beautiful monuments and statues.
  • The Tall Ship: This ship carries a lot of maritime history with it and it is the only one of five Clyde-built ships that is still in the River Clyde.

Edinburgh

Public Transportation

  • Buses, cars, and walking serve as the main forms of transportation in Edinburgh.

Outdoor Attractions

  • Dalkeith County Park: This is a stunning destination with many attractions, ranging from the Fort Douglas Adventure Playground and the Wellbeing Lab, making it a place for all ages to enjoy.
  • Princes Street Gardens: This spectacular garden immerses visitors with vibrant scenery and allows them to have a moment of peace in the midst of the bustling city.
  • Victoria Street: This street has beautifully colored building left and right plus has vantage points which allow one to a really nice cityscape view.
  • Arthur's Seat: This scenic location is the very definition of tranquility with swans swimming in the loch and breathtaking views of Edinburgh, all while being host to a dormant volcano.
  • The Royal Mile: This street has churches, townhouses, inns, museums, restaurants, and much more, making it always enjoyable for a passerbyer.
  • Water of Leith Walkway: This walkway is a popular cycling and walking route for good reason, as it takes you through some of the prettiest areas of Edinburgh.
  • Royal Botanic Garden: This botanic garden has a great diversity of plants making each area of the garden a fascination to explore.
  • The Meadows: Taking a stroll through the meadows allows for a tranquil experience all while taking in the beautiful architecuture of Edinburgh University's central campus.

Indoor and Historical Attractions

  • St. Giles Cathedral: Embrace the beauty of this Cathedral, as it has a mosaic of stained glass and beautiful stone carvings that would be a shame not to be recognized for the wonders they are.
  • Duddingston Village: This village dates back to the 1300s and is the definition of a perfect tranquil village that many dream of living in one day, as it is just one scenic beauty.
  • National Museum of Scotland: This enchanting museum has much more than meets the eye, with many curiousities within that will surely keep you hooked.
  • Edinburgh Castle: This is arguably Scotland's most famous landmark, which is a beauty by itself and contains more beauties within, as it houses the stunning collection of Crown Jewels.
  • Palace of Holyroodhouse: This is the Queen's official Edinburgh Residence and has been a center of Scottish history, as royal wedding have taken place within its walls.
  • The National Galleries of Scotland: These galleries are home to 65,000+ paintings by Scotland's most famous historic figures, such as Robbie Burns and Sean Connery.
  • Greyfriars Church: This church is Edinburgh's oldest graveyard, which is the final resting place of various famous Scots, such the the poet ALlan Ramsay.
  • The Museum of Childhood: This museum has a top of the line collection of toys, dolls, games and educates viewers of the games of children all throughout Scottish history would have enjoyed.
