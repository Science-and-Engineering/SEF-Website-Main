General Facts About Scotland

Scotland is a part of the United Kingdom. It is home to around 800 small islands, notable ones being the northern isles of Shetland and Orkney, the Hebrides, etc. Scotland is surrounded by a various bodies of waters including the North Sea in the east, the Atlantic Ocean in the north and west, and the Irish Sea in the south-west. Scotland is known for the countries beautiful highlands, vivid nature, and fascinating culture with a rich medieval history. It’s not hard to see why the national animal of Scotland is the Unicorn, as it is truly a magical country.

The main reason why thousands of people travel to Scotland for their summer holiday is: Nature! No matter where you go in Scotland, you should be able to find natural reserves, cycling paths, national parks, scenic areas, geoparks, and many other outdoor events.

Some of the most famous ones are:

  • Loch Ness
  • Golf at St. Andrew's
  • Isle of Arran
  • Riverside Museum
  • Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh
  • The Falkirk Wheel

Major Cities in Scotland

Edinburgh: About 500,000 people live in Edinburgh, but don't let the small population distract you from the city's fascinating history and culture. The best way to explore Edinburgh is on foot because of the small size of the city. Attractions here include the Edinburgh Zoo, which is one of the most renowned zoo's in the world. Edinburgh also has the world famous icon of Scotland, the legendary Edinburgh Castle which houses the Crown Jewels, One O’Clock Gun, Half Moon Battery, and the national war museum and memorial. There are many historical monuments including the Scott Monument, Nelson Monument, and the National Monument of Scotland. If you are interested in Medieval Culture and more of the history related part of Scotland you can visit the various castles including the Lauriston Castle, Craigmillar Castle, and obviously the Edinburgh Castle.
