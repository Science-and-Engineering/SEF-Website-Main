Scottish Entertainment

Film

With Scotlands stunning and captivating landscape, it’s no wonder why it attracts some of the world’s leading filmmakers to film in its landscape. The Scottish highlands provide the perfect backdrop for many movie scenes, and example being the Harry Potter train scenes being filmed in the Glenfinnan Viaduct. Loch Leven, Glencoe, and the Mamores mountains provided the backdrop for the movie Braveheart. Additionally, it’s hosted movies with some of the most top brass names in Hollywood, such as Brad Pitt, Tom Hardy, Scarlett Johansson, Daniel Craig, etc.

Sports

A lot of popular international sports can look to Scotland to find the country of origin, including soccer, gold, hockey, rugby, tennis, and cricket. Scottish sports can get very competitive, making it way more fun. Sports such as shinty, curling, and obviously the unique Scottish Highland Games can provide for another pleasurable activity that will appeal those who are looking for different types of recreational things and will provide them a good time. Additionally, the world class facilities, biking trails, and practice grounds provide the perfect place for people to hone in on their skills to take them to the next level.

Scottish Festivals

If you are lucky enough to be around in Scotland in time for some of their world-renowned festivals, then you’ll definitely have a blast. Here are some of many festivals Scotland hosts:

  • Celtic Connections Glasgow (January)
  • Glasgow Film Festival (February)
  • Inverness Music Festival (March)
  • Beltane Fire Festival (April)
  • Orkney Folk Festival (May)
  • Wildhood Festival (June)
  • Lomond Folk Festival (July)
  • Edinburgh International Festival (August)
  • Flavor of Shetland (September)
  • EH6 Festival, Leith Edinburgh (October)
  • Colours of Cluny Festival (November)
  • Edinburgh Hogmanay (December)
